D. Mora Kitola
      Mora Kitola, an open mora out side Hulme walled town is one of
the most important ritual moras in the Dhokato cluster and including
the whole Karate Region. It is an annual communal assembly mora for
the whole of Karate Region. As informants say, the Karate Katana-Aba
Timba, elders and generation leaders gather at this mora once in a year,
on the month of January, to discuss matters related to the successes
and/or failures in Karate Region. It is here that the people of the Karate
Region take blessing from the Aba Timba. Here, the Aba Timba prays for
rain, good order and fertility of the society.
      On the other hand, this mora, every 18 years the generation grades
of Hirba and Kalkusa erect Olayta trees to commemorate their
generation.
      During the Kara ceremony the last ritual dance of the three sister
towns takes place at this mora before it is concluded at Mora Kolalta,
where it started. During this ceremony the killed ritual animal’s skin is
hung on a big acacia tree at center of this mora.
