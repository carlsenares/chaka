No new terraces are constructed today. But, it is not unusual to see, today,

B- The Walled Towns (Paleta)

The Konso live in dry stone walled towns (Paleta) located on high hills selected
for their strategic and defensive advantage. These towns are circled by,
between one and six rounds of dry stone defensive walls. As described by
Hallpike (1974) “… they crown the summit of a hill, or are built on a spur, so
that the terrain falls away steeply on three sides, leaving only the level ground
of the fourth to be especially heavily defended”. The three steep sides are
usually bordered by terrace farms. Access to the town is possible only
through one side. It is only at this fourth side, outside of the walls, that
current development pressures as they are presented in the form of schools,
clinics, flour mills, churches and modern burials could be observed. There is
some times a groove of dense trees of euphorbia and finger-cactus between
the walls and the terraces. This groove is known as Dina, and every town has
Dina that is used as a source for fire wood and as a latrine for the community.
