      Further more, moras have additional functions. Young adults are
expected not to know, hear or see what their parents do at nights; so
after dinner all young males go to moras to pass the night together in the
Pafta, men’s sleeping house. The Pafta are constructed within the mora
on stone plat form that attain ~80cm high( Fig. 6). They are built on
thick poles (between 8 and 12) of hard wood. The niche is located in the
higher section 100cm-150cm above the ground lined with timber from
one to the other end. A small opening serving as a gate in to the niche is
left in the middle just above the central pole which also serve as a footing
