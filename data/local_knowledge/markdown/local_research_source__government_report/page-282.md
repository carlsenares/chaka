Objective Three:
To insure that research on Konso culture is used to educate the
community and the information is included in the educational
program of the local schools.
   - The Konso culture has been subject to various research works but
      none of these results were communicated to the community.
   - The importance of the traditional knowledge of the Konso may be
      neglected through time unless communicated to the young.
   - Informing the community about the importance of the values of
      their culture in comparison to other similar cultures can provide
      them with a sense of belongingness to the greater global cultures.
      This could provide them an additional drive to conserve their
      cultural landscape.

The strategy to achieve the above include:
   a. Presentation of research results to the community in the form of
      posters, exhibitions, conferences create awareness that in turn
      help as a conservation drive.
   b. Education through museum display of cultural objects and their
      presentation to the community and outsiders.
   c. Introductory texts on the value of the Konso cultural landscape
      can be prepared.

Objective Four:
To provide for a system for heritage presentation which insure its
protection and satisfy visitor interest.
