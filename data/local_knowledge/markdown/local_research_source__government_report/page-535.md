D.    Daga-hela (generation transfer marking stones/stele)

The Konso people have an age grading system through which every
generation takes responsibility to protect the well being of its community.
This system is linked with a tradition of erecting a generation marking stone
called Daga-hela. Daga-hela is a freshly quarried columnar basalt reaching
three meters tall, transported and erected through a ritual process.
Generations who have well served the community ( for example, protecting
their town from fire and enemies, conserving the walls and the Moras), based
on the evaluation of the elders could have the honor of having their
generation marker stone Daga-Hela erected for them near the most
prestigious Mora, as witness for their good did and inspiration for younger
generations. Less successful generations have their stones erected else where
in the town or outside the walls. The latter are usually smaller in size. The
erection of the Daga-hela is done following a two months ritual ceremony
called the Kara.
