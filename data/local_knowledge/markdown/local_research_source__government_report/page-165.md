III.4.1. Traditional terraces
     The Konso use blocks of locally abundant basalt interlocked
with smaller blocks stabilized by soil. These terraces retain the soil
from erosion and create terrace saddles that are used for
agriculture. The terraces are the main features of the Konso
Landscape and the hills are contoured by the dry stone terraces
that could reach at some places up to 8 m high and 12 meters
wide. The terrace retention walls are built with heavier blocks at
the base. The saddles that are prepared for agriculture are between
four and eight meters wide at most places.
