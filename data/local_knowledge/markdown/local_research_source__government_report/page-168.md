III.4.2. Walled towns (Paleta)
     The Konso live in stone walled towns (Paleta) located on high
ground   locations    selected   for   their   strategic   and   defensive
advantage. These towns are circled by, between one and six rounds
of dry stone defensive walls. As described by Hallpike, “… they [the
towns]crown the summit of a hill, or are built on a spur, so that the
terrain falls away steeply on three sides, leaving only the level
ground of the fourth to be especially heavily defended”. The three
steep sides are usually bordered by terrace farms. The last side
which is leveled is through which major access to the town is
possible. It is only at this fourth side, outside of the walls, that
current development pressures as they are presented in the form of
schools, clinics, flower mills, churches and modern burials are
observed.
