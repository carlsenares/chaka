The community members of the generation in power are alert and sleep
together, at night, in the Pafta within the Mora space to putout fire. Every
walled town plants euphorbia and finger cactus in the Dina that they use

The traditional system is responding appropriately so far to natural

(iv) Visitor/tourism pressure

Visitors to Konso are usually passers-by who stop for a few hours on their
way to the Southern Omo region. It is expected that, in the future this will
change and more visitors may stay longer; as the landscape is open and
the   properties   are   diverse.   There   has   not   been   any   problem
accommodating tourists. Nevertheless, tourists visiting the traditional
walled towns put a certain amount of psychological         pressure on the
elderly community members.

From the current experience, the walled towns can accommodate up to
four groups of 10 to 15 guided visitors at a time. This number could be
accommodated without adverse effects provided that the tourist experience
is aided by trained local guides.
