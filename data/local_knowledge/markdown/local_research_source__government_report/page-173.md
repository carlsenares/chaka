b - Homestead organization
       Each individual’s homestead has its own dry stonewall and wood
fenced compound called ohida. The individual compound is around
400m2 area planted mainly with Morinaga and coffee trees.                   As
Hallpike (1972:33) says, individual homesteads "... are extremely
cramped, the roofs of the huts touching and overlapping each other in
many     cases".   Hallpike   (2000:50)   continues,     each    individual’s
“…compound is surrounded by a high fence of wooden palings…
[Besides] the compounds are in most cases adjacent to one another, with
their fences acting as a party wall. They are divided into blocks by the
lanes that wind between them.”
