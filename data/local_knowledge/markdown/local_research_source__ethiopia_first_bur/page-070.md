Methane Emissions

Methodological Issues

The 2006 IPCC methodology, Tier 1 method was used to calculate methane (CH4) emission from manure
management using Equation 10.22 according to 2006 IPCC Guideline.

The activity data used to estimate methane (CH4) emission from manure management was collected from
Ethiopia Central Statistics service. Default emission factor from 2006 IPCC Guideline table 10.14 and 10.15

Nitrous Oxide Emissions

There are both direct and indirect N2O emissions from manure that occur after excretion. Indirect
emissions of N2O result from volatile N losses in the form of ammonia (NH3) and nitric oxides (NOx)
that are lost during manure storage and management and deposited somewhere else, where those
subsequently can lead to additional N2 O emissions. The Tier1 calculations of N2O emissions from manure
management are based on the total amount of N excreted from all livestock categories that is managed in
different MMS and which is multiplied with emission factors for each MMS.
