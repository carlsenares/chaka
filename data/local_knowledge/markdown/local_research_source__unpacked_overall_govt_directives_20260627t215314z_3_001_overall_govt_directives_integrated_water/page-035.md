Solar irradiance is expressed in W/m2 and the total amount in J/m2. Conversion between the currently
used unit (SI) and the former unit (calories) can be performed using the following formulae:
            Solar irradiance: 1 kW/m2 = 1.433 cal/cm2/min
            Total amount of solar radiation: 1 MJ/m2 = 23.89 cal/cm2
A radiometer is used to determine solar radiation. A radiometer absorbs solar radiation at its sensor,
transforms it into heat, and measures the resulting amount of heat energy to ascertain the level of solar
radiation. The radiometers used for ordinary observation are pyrheliometers and pyranometers that
measure direct solar radiation and global solar radiation, respectively.

Evaporation
Evaporation can be measured with the help of a Piche tube evaporimeter (mL) at a height of 2 m from
the ground surface (Stevenson screen) twice a day at 0900 and 1800. It can also be measured with the
help of class-A evaporation pans (mm) at the ground surface. Alternatively, potential evaporation can
be calculated using Penman–Monteith or Hargreaves equations.
Instrument: Piche evaporimeter and class-A evaporation pans
Frequency: Twice a day at 0900 and 1800 (for Piche evaporimeter)
                   Daily at 0900 and 1500 (for class-A evaporation pans)
