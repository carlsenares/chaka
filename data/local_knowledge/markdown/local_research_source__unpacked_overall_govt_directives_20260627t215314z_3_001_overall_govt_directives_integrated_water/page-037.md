5.3.3 Land use and land cover
Land use/cover data are required for many applications, such as local land use planning, development of
national agricultural strategies, analysis of crop/erosion/hydrology model input parameters (for example,
C and P factors of erosion modeling), analysis of climate change impacts, monitoring of national targets for
land restoration and land degradation neutrality, water resource development and management, natural
hazards assessments and mitigations, and so on. Nationally, only a few land use/cover datasets exist, such
as those produced by the Ethiopian Highlands Reclamation Study in the 1980s, the Woody Biomass Inventory
and Strategic Planning Project in the 1990s, a land use and land cover (LULC) map developed by FAO in the
1980s, and recent datasets developed by the Water and Land Resource Center (WLRC) (Hurni et al., 2015).
