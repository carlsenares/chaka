Rainfall direction
Rainfall direction can be determined using an inclinometer, which computes the average weighted rainfall
direction of each storm. Thus, each storm is represented by a three-dimensional vector, representing
the mean angle and direction of daily rainfall (SCRP, 2000) (Table 7).
Instrument: Inclinometer
Frequency: For each storm

Temperature
The heat balance difference in the atmosphere between regions creates temperature distribution.
This temperature distribution generates wind currents along with cloud and rainfall phenomena. Thus,
atmospheric temperature is one of the most important meteorological elements as well as wind and
precipitation.
Temperature should be recorded daily by reading minimum and maximum thermometers at a height of
1.5 m from the ground surface (for air temperature, Stevenson screen) and 5 cm above the soil surface
(for surface temperature, under shelter) (SCRP, 2000) (Table 8). In addition to the thermometer on the
Stevenson screen, temperature data can be recorded using an automatic weather station. It is best to
set up the automatic recording at 15-min intervals to align with the National Meteorological Service
Agency dataset.
Instrument: Thermometer
Frequency: Daily for Stevenson screen: minimum temperature at 0900 and maximum temperature at
1800 and 15-min intervals for an automatic weather station.
