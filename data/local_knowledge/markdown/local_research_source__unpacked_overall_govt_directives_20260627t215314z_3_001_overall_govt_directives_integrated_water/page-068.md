5.4.8 Costs of SWC structures/interventions
Watershed-based interventions should be sustainable in terms of social, economic, and environmental
aspects. To enhance sustainable adoption and spread, introduced technologies and practices should be
affordable to watershed users. Thus, it is important to know the costs of implementing an SWC technology
or any management practice to estimate its benefits/profits. WOCAT questionnaires can be used to
record the costs of technologies (WOCAT, 2016). If possible, break down the costs of establishment/
initial investment and maintenance/recurrent costs according to Table 36. If you are unable to break
down the costs, estimate the total costs of establishing/maintaining the technology (Table 37). Costs
and inputs specified should refer to the actual technology area/technology unit applied in a watershed.
