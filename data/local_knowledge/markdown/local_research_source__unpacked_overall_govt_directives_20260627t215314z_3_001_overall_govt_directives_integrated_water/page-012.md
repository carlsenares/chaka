Guideline for Integrated Watershed Management Data Collection




Glossary of Terms
                 Baseflow        River flow without surface runoff.

              Bathymetry         The measurement of the depths of lakes, reservoirs, dams, and other
                                 large bodies of water.

                       Data      Information in raw or unorganized form such as measurements or statistics
                                 used as a basis for reasoning, discussion, or calculation.

       Experimental plot         A plot used to evaluate different soil conservation practices and land
                                 use systems.

         Geomorphology           The study of landforms, their processes, form, and sediments at the
                                 surface of the Earth.

             Gully erosion       Form of erosion consisting of open, unstable channels that have been
                                 cut more than 0.3 m deep into the ground.

                 Landslide       Movement of a mass of rock, debris, or earth down a slope.

       Local knowledge           A collection of facts and information related to the entire system of
                                 concepts, beliefs, and perceptions that people hold about the locality
                                 around them. This includes the way people observe and measure their
                                 surroundings, how they solve problems, and how they validate new
                                 information.

             Mapping unit        The smallest mapping area where there are homogeneous land features
                                 such as slope, soil, land cover, geomorphology, etc.

                 Metadata        Data that provide information about other data; in short, data about data.

             Pluviometer         Equipment used to record rainfall continuously on a recording chart.

               Rill erosion      Small channel of linear erosion resulting from concentrated overland flow.

                     Runoff      An overland flow of excess rainfall.
