Foreword
I am delighted to present this national guideline for standardizing integrated watershed management
(IWM) data collection methods developed through the collaboration of national and international
researchers. This guideline represents a significant milestone in the advancing and critical evaluation
of IWM practices. Its development was initiated by the Coalition of the Willing (CoW) and facilitated with
support from EIAR, CIAT, and GIZ. The Ethiopian Institute of Agricultural Research extends its deepest
gratitude to all contributors for their dedication and teamwork in bringing this initiative to fruition.
The EIAR acknowledges this guideline as a critical addition to a broader series of data standardization
guidelines. These include guidelines on agricultural water management, soil microbiology, soil fertility,
and soil surveys, which have already been published and adopted by various stakeholders. These
initiatives collectively play a pivotal role in harmonizing data.
This IWM guideline focuses on key areas such as information/parameters to be collected, instructions
for data collection, and measurement and analysis techniques covering watershed delineation,
characterization, design, implementation, monitoring and evaluation, and impact assessment stages.
Building on prior initiatives, the IWM guideline stands as an exemplary model for other sectors in agricultural
research. It demonstrates a well-structured approach to data standardization and underscores the value
of collaboration among experts from diverse fields. This collective effort paves the way for creating
an integrated system for generating, storing, and sharing agricultural data efficiently and effectively.
The EIAR is dedicated to incorporating this guideline into its operations and advocating for its adoption
across the national agricultural research system. On behalf of the EIAR, I express sincere gratitude to all
contributors and reviewers who have invested their time and expertise in this initiative. Your invaluable
contributions have been instrumental in creating a resource that will greatly enhance our capacity for
data standardization and use. We firmly believe that this guideline will become an essential tool for
researchers, practitioners, and policymakers, enabling them to unlock the transformative potential of
data in integrated watershed management.


                                                                                 Temesgen Desalegn (PhD)
                                                                             Director, Soil and Water Research
                                                                   Ethiopian Institute of Agricultural Research
