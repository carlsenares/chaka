i. Manual sampling strategies
Discharge and sediment concentration measurements using manual methods are very difficult for large
streams or rivers characterized by flash flood. Discharge and sediment concentration measurement
should consider the likely behavior of the river and the timing of significant concentration fluctuations.
This is the case when rain is falling frequently and causing high flow fluctuation in the stream/river and
fluctuation of sediment concentration.
The procedure for manual sampling involves measuring
X      Water level (using a staff gauge)
X      Stream velocity (using a floating or current meter)
X      Discharge (using velocity area methods)
X      Sediment concentration
      »       Water sampling (using manual sampling)
      »       Filtration (to extract the sediment)
      »       Analysis of grain size


ii. Automatic sampling equipment and monitoring
Upstream soil erosion and downstream sediment yield are linked/related to each other by sediment
delivery ratio (SDR). The SDR is affected by the location of sediment source areas in the watershed,
topographic characteristics, vegetation cover, and climate.
Procedure for automatic measurement:
X      An automatic sampler is used to take runoff and sediment samples during a specific time interval.
X      Sediment yield will be calculated from the discharge and sediment concentration (Table 24).
X      The sampling scale for sediment yield measurement can be at any scale: micro-watershed, sub-
       catchment; the measurement accuracy error ranges from 5% to 10%.
