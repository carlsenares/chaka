5.3.16 Soil moisture content
Soil moisture content plays a vital role in watershed productivity by directly impacting agricultural yield,
livestock grazing capacity, and forest health. This moisture content influences hydrological, ecological,
climatological, and atmospheric watershed processes.
To accurately measure soil moisture content across a watershed (Table 25):
1.     Divide the watershed based on land use, slope, geology, and soil characteristics.
2.     Collect soil samples through either
      »       Gravimetric sampling: taking 100–200-g soil samples every 20-cm depth and weighing before
              and after oven-drying at 105 °C for 24 hours without a cover to remove all water in the soil (Taye
              et al., 2024).
      »       Electronic monitoring: using TDR probes and data loggers for real-time moisture tracking at
              various depths.
