NATIONAL STANDARD



Sediment sampling
The clear portion of the runoff collected in collector 1 (tank 1) should be siphoned out carefully. The
remaining sediment must be stirred thoroughly and weighed together with sediment deposited on a ditch
and a known amount of sediment sample taken for gravimetric moisture content determination (Tables
21 and 22). The samples must be oven-dried at 105 oC for 24 hours. The volume of runoff in collector 2
(tank 2) must also be measured, stirred thoroughly, and a 1-L sample taken for sediment concentration
analysis. Sediment concentration multiplied by the total volume of runoff is used to estimate the total
soil losses from each rainstorm.
Total soil loss (ST) is calculated as
                                                ST = SD + SA + SB
where ST = weight of total soil loss calculated
        SD = weight of dry soil loss collected from runoff and sediment collecting ditch
        SA = weight of dry soil loss recorded in tank 1
        SB = weight of dry soil loss calculated in tank 2
                                                  SD = SDW x SDO
where SDW = weight of wet soil loss recorded on runoff and sediment collecting ditch
        SDO = oven-dried soil percentage of soil sample from runoff collecting ditch
                                                 SA = SAW x SAO
where SAW = weight of wet soil loss recorded in Tank 1
        SAO = oven-dried soil percentage of soil sample in Tank 1
                                                   SB = RT x RC
where RC = runoff sediment concentration in Tank 1
Instruments: Bounded experimental plot, slot divisor, runoff collecting tanks, sediment collecting ditch,
measuring stick, graduated cylinder, sediment sampling bottles, filter paper, beakers, balance, oven,
markers.
