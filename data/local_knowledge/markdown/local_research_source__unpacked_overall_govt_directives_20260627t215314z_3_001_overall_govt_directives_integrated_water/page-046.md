5.3.9 Soil erosion measurement using runoff plot
Runoff plots could be used to measure erosion due to combinations of erosion factors/processes. Use
the following procedures:
X    Use plots of 4 m × 15 m for <20% slope and 4 m × 10 m for >20% slope (representing most erodible
     soils; major land covers and slope classes).
X    Select desired plot locations for the different soil types, vegetation cover, and topographic features.
X    Care should be taken not to compact, disturb, and translocate the soil during plot installation, in
     particular around the plot border or boundary.
X    Required instrumentation:
     »     Bounding material metal plate or wooden or compacted soil bund or concrete wall.
     »     Gutter or inlet tube to convey runoff water and sediment from the plot to storage.
     »     Collecting tank volume determined based on maximum runoff and sediment volume expected
           from the plot. The collector tanks can be two or more depending on the event runoff volume
           generated from runoff plots.
     »     Runoff and sediment sampling devices.
     »     Lab facilities to determine sediment concentration.
X    Runoff sampling procedures for collecting runoff from the plot and sediment sampling procedure
     include the following:
     »     Mix the runoff with the sediment to ensure uniform distribution of the sediment and sample
           representativeness.
     »     Prepare runoff and sediment sampling devices.
     »     Collect 1-L-depth integrated runoff and sediment sample.
     »     Filter the sample to determine sediment concentration.
X    Frequency of sampling depends on the purpose of measurement: storm-/event-based sampling or
     daily sampling. Daily sampling is usually enough for soil erosion measurement for watershed studies.
X    Monitor changes in plot characteristics such as vegetation cover percentage, management such as
     cropping and crop growth stage, percentage ground cover, etc.
X    Soil loss due to sheet and rill erosion from the plot is calculated based on the runoff volume, sediment
     concentration, plot area, and sampling duration.
X    Uncertainties are related to soil erosion measurements. Therefore, measurement accuracy is
     important, considering the different sources of measuring errors.
