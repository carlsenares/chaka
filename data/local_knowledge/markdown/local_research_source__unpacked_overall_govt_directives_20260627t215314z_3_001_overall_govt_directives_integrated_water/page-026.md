5.2.2 Infrastructure (roads, settlements, and social services)
Infrastructure (roads, settlements, etc.) development often affects runoff patterns of landscapes and could
cause problems in landscapes, such as flooding, gully erosion, sediment delivery, and other cascading
effects. If managed, runoff water from such infrastructure could be used for productive purposes. There
is therefore a need to evaluate infrastructure-landscape interactions in watershed development and
management. The approach/method to be followed includes (a) an inventory of existing infrastructure
and services (roads, settlements, etc.); (b) evaluation of the effects/damages of runoff water from such
infrastructure on the landscape (gullies, flooding, landslides, etc.) and vice versa; and (c) assessments
of the possibilities for managing runoff water from such infrastructure for use for various purposes,
dissipation to safe locations, and/or design of mitigation measures. Table 4 summarizes the data and
key parameters to be documented and evaluated.
