NATIONAL STANDARD



X   Integrating scientific and local knowledge: In this transdisciplinary approach, indigenous practices
    are considered in the research work and local stakeholders are engaged/involved. Pre-existing
    technologies and local indigenous knowledge should be used as an entry point to integrated watershed
    management. This helps to articulate and convert implicit knowledge into explicit knowledge.
    This could be done at all levels of IWM: during inception/planning, design/technology selection,
    implementation, and monitoring and impact evaluations.
X   Data interpretation: All data should be carefully interpreted, because each method has its specific
    range of applications. An application of data beyond their specific range should be avoided, as this
    might lead to unreliable information, thus misleading stakeholders and decision-makers in various
    aspects, including prioritizing interventions and finding appropriate nature-based solutions.
X   Selection of methods: The methods described in this guideline are assumed to be widely known
    and easily understandable and usable by experts from different disciplines with average technical
    knowledge in the subject areas. For example, the geological and hydrogeological parameters are
    determined by experts in the field. In this guideline, only limited methods and approaches are presented
    because many users have already been working in poor research facilities and this challenge is
    expected in using this guideline. Alternative methods are presented whenever relevant to provide
    more options for users depending on their working conditions (technical and research facilities).
    More advanced and sophisticated methods and approaches are not adequately presented here as
    the main aim of this guideline is to develop standardized data generation methods that could be
    used by most of the research and development personnel in the country. This ultimately provides an
    opportunity to build a national dataset on the subject matters that is adequate to inform evidence-
    based decision-making and promote better watershed management practices.
