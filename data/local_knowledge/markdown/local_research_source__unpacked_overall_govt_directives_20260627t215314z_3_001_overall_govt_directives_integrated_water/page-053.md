c.   Grid-square method
     »   For reservoirs that become dry at the end of the dry season, the water stored in the reservoir
         is usually completely used for irrigation and livestock consumption. Therefore, it is possible to
         measure sediment thickness by using regularly spaced profile pits or by auguring.
     »   The volume of sediment could be calculated with the following procedures:
             Measure sediment thickness using meter sticks by opening profile pits per reservoir along
              transects (Figure 11).
             The number of pits will be determined by the size and nature of the original ground level of
              the reservoir. If the original ground level is rugged at the time of construction, the thickness
              of the deposited sediment is also expected to vary.
             Map deposited sediment boundary, location of pits (Figure 12), and topography of the
              current ground level (bottom of the reservoir) using surveying instruments (theodolite or
              others such as Total Station).
             Delimit the boundary of the sediment deposition area (thickness = 0) by digitizing the
              surveyed points as a polygon in IDRISI software.
             Produce a DEM in GIS with X and Y readings taken using surveying instruments, taking
              measured sediment thickness as the z value.
             Sediment volume is then determined by running the EXTRACT module in IDRISI®, which
              extracts summary statistics from an existing image file (Haregeweyn et al., 2006; Tamene
              et al., 2011).
