Measuring tillage erosion
Nyssen et al. (2000) developed a method to calculate tillage erosion rate using a traditional “ard” plow in
Ethiopia (Figure 8), and the same method is recommended, which includes the following:
X      Identify areas where tillage erosion is to be measured.
X      Number stones and hide them in the soil but measure the location of each of the stones from a given
       reference point.
X      Allow the farmers to till their land normally (without knowing where these stones are).
X      Find the new locations of the stones and measure the distances of these stones from the reference
       point.
X      Calculate the distance that these rocks are translocated by tillage passes (Figure 9).
X      This provides the rate of translocation of geological materials due to tillage.



5.3.13 Sediment yield
Sediment yield is the mass of sediment crossing a specific field boundary, watershed, catchment, or
basin and is expressed in units of mass of sediment per unit area and time, which is also called specific
sediment yield.
Sediment yield can be measured using
1.     Fluvial monitoring
2.     Reservoir survey
3.     Application of a sediment yield model
Fluvial monitoring is a continuous process of measuring discharge and sediment concentration within
the discharge. Data on sediment yield at the outlet of the river basin reveal the rate of erosion and soil
loss of the upstream watershed.
