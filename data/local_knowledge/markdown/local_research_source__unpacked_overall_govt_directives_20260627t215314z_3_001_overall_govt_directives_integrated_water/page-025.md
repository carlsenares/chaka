NATIONAL STANDARD



5.2.1 Data on constraint analysis of watersheds
Watershed issues and constraints can be identified and prioritized by means of interviews, focus group
discussions (gender and wealth rank disaggregated), and field observations through transect walks. At each
interval of the transect survey (every 75 to 100 m along the transect depending on the heterogeneity of the
watershed attributes such as land use, soil, vegetation, etc.), all biophysical and socioeconomic information
must be recorded. Respondents must represent different community categories (such as age groups, from
different villages, wealth status, and gender). The participants identify constraints of a social, economic, and
environmental nature from broader dimensions, along with technical, legal, governance, and financial aspects.
Discuss how to prioritize and screen the constraints to come up with a manageable number of constraints for
pairwise ranking (Tables 1, 2, and 3).
