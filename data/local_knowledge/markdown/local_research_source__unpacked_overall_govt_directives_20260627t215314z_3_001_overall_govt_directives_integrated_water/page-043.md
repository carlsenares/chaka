When bigger runoff plots are used, the size of the collector can’t accommodate the runoff; therefore, multi-
slot-divisor runoff collectors should be used (Figure 5). There should be two or more runoff collectors.
One-ninth of the runoff from the first runoff collector and one-fifth from the second collector are directed
to the collector tanks using slot divisors. The central slot divisors are connected to collection tanks
through tubes. The runoff collected through the single-slot divisor in each tank should be multiplied
by the number of slot divisors and, finally, the total runoff from all collectors should be summed up to
calculate the total runoff depth from a plot.
