NATIONAL STANDARD



To understand upstream and downstream connectivity or interaction of SWC practices with slope profile,
homogeneous sub-catchments can be further divided into upper, middle, and lower landscape positions.
Sample at least three consecutive SWC measures at each position (bunds, trenches, drainage ditches,
hedgerows, or grass strips, etc.). Measure design parameters (length, width, height, depth, gradient),
layout parameters (spacing, vertical interval, density/count), sediment retention (depth and volume),
and age and level of damage to structures, and take into account maintenance measures. Take spacing/
vertical interval data in each sample structure to estimate linear SWC structure density.
Methods: To measure sediment retention of bunds, detailed sampling along the structure is required.
Sample three consecutive structures per landscape position (Table 27). Mark 30 m in length on each
structure and take measurements at three points at 10-m intervals, measuring selected parameters such
as dimension, sediment accumulation, and level of damage. Measure five times during the rainy season:
twice early immediately after crop planting, twice mid-season, and once at the end of the season.
Accumulated sediment represents a portion of soil loss from the terraced area that would otherwise
flow downslope. To measure the amount of sediment accumulated/retained by the structures, two
independent methods can be used:
1.   For structures with runoff drainage ditches on the upper side (e.g., graded soil bunds and stone bunds).
2.   For those without runoff ditches (e.g., fanya juu, grass strip, bench terrace).
For structures with ditches, sediment accumulation is measured along the ditch on the upper side of
the bunds. In each sample point at 10-m intervals of 30 m in total length, first we assume/estimate
the original dimension of the ditch (depth and width) and then measure the depth of the remaining
top part of the ditch (i.e., depth above the accumulated sediment) and subtract it from the original
depth of the ditch to estimate the amount of sediment retained or accumulated on ditches. This
amount is a fraction of the soil loss washed from the terraced area and part of the total soil loss
washed down and transported out along the gradient. The volume of sediment accumulated per unit
length of structure can then be estimated by multiplying the depth of the sediment by the cross-
sectional area of the ditch.
For those structures without a ditch, sediment retained/accumulated along the embankment of structures
could be measured by either of the following two methods:
1.   Before the start of rain, lay out a 1-m plastic sheet at three points (center of 10-m intervals) along a
     30-m-long sediment accumulation area. Measure the volume of sediment using buckets or weight
     of sediment every 2 weeks until the end of the rainy season.
2.   Before the start of rain, install enough graduated/marked wooden pegs or iron pins at 10-m
     intervals along the 30-m length (with a minimum of three pegs per interval). Measure the
     depth of accumulated sediment every 2 weeks until the end of the rainy season. The volume of
     sediment retained is estimated by multiplying the average depth by the sediment accumulation
     area (Tables 28 and 29).
In cases where baseline information is not available on sediment accumulation efficiency of structures
that were constructed some years back, we can reconstruct the slope profile change (before construction
of bunds and after some years) induced by SWC structures in each landscape position (upper, middle,
and lower) (Figure 14). We can then estimate the sediment retained per year and change in sediment
retained between two periods.
