5.4.5 Crop and fodder productivity
Information on the effects of watershed interventions on crop productivity needs to be collected to
determine the impact of SWC practices. This information can be sampled at different parts of the
watershed representing heterogeneity in slope, soil, altitude, and crop type.
X      Sampling and crop yield data collection: Seasonal (Meher and Belg) harvest samples shall be
       collected for all major crops in the watershed. Sample areas must represent various cultivation
       conditions (different slopes, soil types, and altitudes) and be chosen from fields with average
       crop stand conditions. The sampling will be conducted from at least 150 fixed plots and 150
       non-fixed plots.
       Base maps and farmer parcel maps can be used to guide the sampling process. Each sample plot
       will be assigned a unique plot number and marked on the parcel base map.
       To evaluate the effect of SWC measures on crop productivity, harvest samples will be collected from
       three positions relative to field bunds: (1) position a – above the bund, (2) position b – between two
       successive bunds/terraces, and (3) position c – below the bund.
       At each position, all plants, including weeds, will be collected in either a 3 m × 3 m or 2 m × 2 m area
       (Taye et al., 2024).
       The plants will be harvested and grains with covers will be separated by hand and sun-dried for about
       20 days. Straw will be weighed and recorded separately for each sample area.
       Dried grains will be separated from covers by hand and final grain weights will be recorded on the
       harvest sample form per sample area. The grains and straw will be given back to the field owners.
       Additional data collection includes documenting agronomic and crop cultivation information such
       as planting time, previous crops, and tillage frequency. Crop yield data (grain and biomass) will be
       collected, organized, and presented according to year, season, crop type, and intra-terrace areas/
       slope positions.
X      Monitoring fodder biomass production: Samples of biomass harvest of fodder on bunds from 5-m
       length of bunds and 2 m × 2 m sample area from area closures, treated gullies, and grassland will be
       collected two times yearly (in two seasons, June and October) (Tables 33 and 34). Fresh weight will
       be directly measured and recorded.
