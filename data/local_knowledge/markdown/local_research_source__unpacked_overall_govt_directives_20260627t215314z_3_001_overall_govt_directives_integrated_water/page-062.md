Guideline for Integrated Watershed Management Data Collection



5.4.2 Evaluation of SWC technologies using experimental plot
This guideline uses SCRP experimental plot setup and soil loss and runoff data collection methods. The
effects of soil conservation measures or land management practices on soil loss, runoff, soil fertility, crop
yield, and biomass production can be tested against control plots representing local practices without
SWC measures vs experimental plots (EP) of 180 m2 (6 m × 30 m) each. This plot size has been found to
be more appropriate in Ethiopian highlands, where land size is more fragmented. Under very distinct
conditions such as in the dryland and low-rainfall areas where the slope is relatively flat, allowing for
wider spacing between structures/management practices, and when comparing several management
practices (for example, conservation agriculture practices), the plot size can be flexible, with at least
three conservation structures/bunds included. In flat areas, a plot size of 4-m width by 45-m length
could be adequate.
Methods: Measurements can be taken of daily runoff and soil loss after rain for plots with different SWC
measures or practices (Table 30). Each plot is bounded by corrugated iron borders inserted 10 cm in
the ground and 20 cm above the ground to control splash loss and lateral flow, thus enabling runoff to
be collected in the tank through an inlet tube. Plots are established near the rainfall recorder to link the
results of rainfall and runoff periods. Runoff and sediments are collected in two 200–250-L tanks (Figure
15). In most of the cases, a multi-slot devisor (with 9 slots) was used where 1/9 of the runoff entered to
the first tank. When the first thank filled up, runoff flow to the second tank. The volume of runoff water
is measured directly from the depth of runoff in both tanks and the total runoff is nine times the runoff
volume collected from the two tanks. Representative runoff and soil samples are taken for laboratory
analysis. To measure soil losses, collect 500 g of soil and 1 L of suspended sediment samples from tank
A and the overflow tank B, if necessary. The collection system is cleaned after each sampling. The 1-L
sample, taken for sediment assessment, is filtered and dried. The 500-g sample taken from each plot is
also dried in the oven and weighed.
