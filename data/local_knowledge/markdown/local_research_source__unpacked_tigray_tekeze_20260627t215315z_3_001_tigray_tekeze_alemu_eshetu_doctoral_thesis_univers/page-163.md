pull factors (Black et al., 2011). Most notably, disruption of agricultural productivity due to
climate extremes significantly increases migration from developing countries (Falco et al.,

smallholder farmer agricultural production and can potentially lead to livelihood losses and
subsequent migration out of rural areas (Falco et al., 2019; Hermans & Mcleman, 2021). In
developing countries, where agriculture is the primary livelihood source, migration and
remittance will affect it positively or negatively (Huy & Nonneman, 2016; Tong et al., 2019;
Nguyen et al., 2019; Abebaw et al., 2019). Improving agricultural productivity also requires
agricultural technology adoption and sustainable conservation and management of natural
