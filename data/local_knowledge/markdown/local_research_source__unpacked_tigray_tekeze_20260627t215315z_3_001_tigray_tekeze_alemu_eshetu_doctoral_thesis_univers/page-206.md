7.2.4. Empirical Methods
7.2.4.1. PSM method
Because household participation in seasonal migration is not random decision, a simple
comparison of migrant and none migrant households based on their SLM intensity leads to
biased estimation. The decision of household heads and its members to migrate or to stay in
the village could be affected by unobserved factors that may also influence the adoption of
multiple yield-enhancing SLM techniques. Consequently, directly comparing these groups
could lead to selection bias and endogeneity. Thus, estimating the effects of seasonal migration
requires creating comparable observations from the treatment and control groups.
To address this, we consider migration (Mi) as the treatment variable. Migrant is defined as an
individual member of the household living outside the village for at least two consecutive
months for employment purposes during one calendar year. Therefore, migration (Mi) =1 if
