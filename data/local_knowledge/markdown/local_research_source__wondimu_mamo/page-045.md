Soil Erodibility (K) factor

The K factor represents the influence of different soil properties to erosion susceptibility of
(Renard et al., 1997). It is the inherent erodibility of the soil at a particular site. The higher K-
factor values indicate the soil‟s higher susceptibility to soil erosion (Adornado et al., 2009). The
value of K is a function of soil texture, organic matter content, soil structure, and profile
permeability (Wischmeier and Smith, 1978; Renard et al., 1997). However, some alternative
equations have been developed that exclude the soil structure and profile permeability in
estimating the K factor values (Benavidez et al., 2018). Similarly, in the present study, the K
factor was calculated by employing indirect method of soil erodibility computation (Sharpley
and Williams, 1990; Williams, 1995; Neitsch et al., 2000; Khassaf and Rammahi, 2018) based
on only soil texture and organic carbon content data. The RUSLE K factor was then derived
based on USLE K formula (Williams, 1995) represented in equation 3.
