Farmers’ perceptions on their relationship with the investors/investment activities, land deal
processes, and impacts were also assessed (Table 2.3). Regarding land deal processes, most
of the respondents explained that they were not consulted when land deals were made, and
hence, the process was not transparent. They recognize the direct impact of large-scale
agricultural investment on their livelihood and mostly emphasized the adverse sides.
However, they were not compensated for the impacts. Among others, the reasons provided
for no compensations were that most of the land was defined as open and common land, that
the farmers didn’t have holding certificates, and a policy that doesn’t recognize the
landholding contexts of the local community. The paradoxes of open-land, communal land,
