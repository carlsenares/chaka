3.6            Bole volume
Bole volume refers to the merchantable part of stem, up to trees first big branch.
Because bole vole rather a technical measure of the stem, it cannot give exact
information on the commercial value of the forest. However bole volume can serve as
an indicator for timber harvesting potential.

Figure 3.10    Tree bole volume by Major LUCC types




The highest mean bole volume can be found in the Natural regenerated forest (93.1 m3
ha−1), following the forest plantation (55.1 m3 ha−1). The forest plantations in Ethiopia
contain approximately 12.7% of the total bole volume stock, mainly located in the
coniferous planted forests (6.8%), followed by the broadleaved planted forests (4.1%)
and planted forests with bamboo (1.7%). The mean bole volumes are rather low in
other land categories varying from 4.7 to 11.3 m3 ha−1. The 79% of the bole volume in
the natural forest strata is stored in the trees with DBH greater than 40 cm.
