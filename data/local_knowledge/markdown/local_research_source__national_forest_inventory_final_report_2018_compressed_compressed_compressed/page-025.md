1.3            The Woody Biomass Inventory
               and Strategic Planning Project
The Woody Biomass Inventory and Strategic Planning Project (WBSIPP) was the first
forest and forest biomass inventory in Ethiopia. Since its completion, the WBISPP has
been used as a reference for country estimates of the Ethiopian forests and its results are
also included in the FAOs Forest Resource Assessment (FRA) for Ethiopia14. In August
1990, the WBISPP started with the scope to map 350,000 km2 (land cover and
vegetation maps), and conduct a woody biomass inventory in an area of over 300,000
km2, (over a quarter of the country’s land area15).
The main objective of the Project was to strengthen strategic forest resource planning
and woody biomass inventory capacity. Other objectives were to identify and manage
the country’s woody biomass resources and to define a strategy for meeting the future
biofuel needs and to decrease the negative impacts of deforestation.
More specifically, the project’s tasks included building a woody biomass management
information system, as an integral part of forestry and land use strategic planning
throughout an inventory and assessment of above-ground woody biomass stock
resources, including natural and man-made forests. Included in the task was a survey
for the estimation of the consumption and use of woody biomass and other biofuel
substances in order to identify the problems and issues with respect to the sustainable
development and consumption of woody biomass resources.
WBISPP16 estimated the forest coverage of Ethiopia to be 59.7 million hectares of which
6.8% is high forest, 49% woodland, 0.2% plantation forest, and 44% shrub land. The
WBSIPP’s results and the WBSIPP Land Use Land Cover map have been considered in
the NFI sampling design phase in order to identify the forested area and to determine
the sampling intensity of the sampling points.
