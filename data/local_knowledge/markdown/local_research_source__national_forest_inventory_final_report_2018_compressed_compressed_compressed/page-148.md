The map reflects the distribution of aboveground woody biomass (AWB) in Ethiopia on
a 30 m resolution (Figure 2). The estimates are not limited to actual forest areas, which
makes it possible to assess woody resources also outside forested areas. Although
visual interpretation results in a good general representation of biomass on a local
scale, statistical uncertainties on a pixel level are high. This is expected for this kind of
mapping approach, since the remote sensing data cannot capture all the variability that
determines the amount of AWB within a Sampling Unit. This is also depicted in the
model evaluations based on the Out-Of-Bag (OOB) score coming from the random
