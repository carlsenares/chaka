The distribution of the trees by diameter classes is fundamental in order to understand the
structure of the forests. In the natural regenerated forest the DBH distribution shows that
the trees with DBH less than 30 cm represent almost 70% of the total number trees. Trees
with DBH less than 15 cm (supposedly young trees) represent approximately 20% of the
density. In the forest plantations, the trees with DBH less than 30 cm represent more than
84% of all standing trees, and those with DBH less than 15 cm are 30% of the total growing
stock. Probably due to the timber management operations, in the forest plantation the trees
with DBH greater than 40 cm are only 7.3% of the total number of trees.

Figure 3.16        Tree frequencies by diameter classes in the Natural regenerated forest




A slightly different trend is observed in the Woodland where the trees with DBH less
than 30 cm constitute 93% of the total number of trees, and the big trees with DBH
greater than 40 cm are only 2%.
The results for stem volume (i.e. percentages of the stem volumes by DBH classes)
show that the big trees with DBH greater than 40 cm constitute 80% of the total
standing stock.
