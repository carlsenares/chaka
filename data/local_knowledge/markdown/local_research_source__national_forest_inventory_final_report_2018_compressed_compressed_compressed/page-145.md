Appendix A12                  Wall-to-wall biomass map of
                              Ethiopia
General Introduction

This report describes the creation of a wall-to-wall biomass map done for Ethiopia
within the National Forest Inventory project. The map is referenced for the year 2016.
The main idea is to use the field inventory data and use it for the calibration of a multi-
sensor satellite data stack, consisting of optical and radar imagery, in order to get
spatially explicit values of biomass. While those estimates are affected by high
uncertainties on a pixel level, and therefore are critical to use for carbon stock
estimates, the map can give important information on the locations where the actual
biomass deviates considerably from the expected one. The spatial information can then
be used for planning of carbon stock enhancements.

Data preparation

NFI data

For the combination of the field and satellite data, the spatial scale and potential
location errors of both datasets has to be taken into account. The sampling unit design
consists of 4 subplots with a respective size of about 2 ha. In order to account for
location errors of the satellite data and the field inventory plots, the 4 subplots have
been aggregated by considering their mean biomass value per Sampling Unit (SU). The
location of the whole SU has therefore been generalized by calculating the center point
of the available subplots. Subsequently, a buffer zone of 300 m radius is created around
this point and the mean value of the subplot biomass measurements is attributed to
that area.
