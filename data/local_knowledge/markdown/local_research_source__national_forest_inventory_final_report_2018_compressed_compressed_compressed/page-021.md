1.1               Biophysical description of Ethiopia
Ethiopia is positioned in the horn of Africa and lies within the tropics between 3º24‘
and 14º53‘ latitude; and 32º42‘ and 48º12‘ longitude3. With an approximate extent of
1,132,000 km2, Ethiopia’s topography extremely varies with a range of elevation
between -148 m below the sea (Dallol Depression) and 4,620 meters above sea level
(Ras Dashen peak).
Approximately 65% (741,000 km2) of the country is considered as lowlands (i.e., land less
than 1,500 m a.s.l.). The high plains or plateaus are located between the Upper Nile valley
and the border with Sudan and South Sudan within an altitude of 1,300 m and 3,000 m
a.s.l. The northwestern and southeastern highlands are separated by the Main Ethiopian Rift
Valley, part of the Great Ethiopian Rift Valley that runs from Syria to Mozambique.
The Rift Valley is characterized by a chain of lakes throughout the greater part of the
Valley. In the western part, the plateau extends from Eritrea in the north to Kenya in the
south.
