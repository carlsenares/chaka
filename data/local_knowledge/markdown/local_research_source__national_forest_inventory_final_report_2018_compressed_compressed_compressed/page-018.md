                     Conclusions
The main conclusions from the NFI initiative are as follows:
•     From the NFI results, there are indications of considerable degradation in Forests
      and OWL. Results show that population pressure is high given that 65.8% of forest
      lands and 58.8% of OWLs are considered as “disturbed” by human activity. In forest
      land, the main cause of disturbance is encroachment by agriculture. Results on the
      basal area in forest lands also indicate that the forests appear to be degraded, with
      basal area (BA) in forests being 13.3 m2ha-1 and in High woodland only 3.8 m2ha-1 1.
      When looking at the basal area results by biome (forest only plots), in Acacia-
      Commiphora, Combretum- Terminalia, Dry Afromontane and Moist Afromontane
      estimates are 6.5, 8.4, 12.4 and 20.7 m2ha-1, respectively. Only Moist Afromontane
      forest has a basal area greater than 20 m2ha-1, a further indication of degradation;

•     All the established sampling units can be considered as permanent and can be
      revisited during the next NFI cycles. Re-measurements of the sampling units will
      allow for better understanding of the status of forests and especially forest
      dynamics in the country. A continuous NFI is recommended and entails a certain
      proportion of sampling units being re-measured every year (as for example in
      Finland and Poland);
•     All collected data are stored in an Open Foris Collect database and managed by the
      MRV staff in the Directorate’s experts at MEFCC. This database can be used for
      further analysis and scientific research purposes. Despite results being reported by
      region, it should be noted that the first NFI was designed to give results primarily
      for the national level. An intensification of NFI sampling in the future can expand
      the use of NFI data to subnational levels and improve the accuracy of the estimates;
•     The implemented NFI methodology can be adapted and improved, by adding new
      variables or stratification schemes. In 2016, Ethiopia already institutionalized MRV
