3.10              Comparisons on the results
The average AGB of trees in Ethiopian natural forest land is estimated at 125 tons ha−1
and in OWL 7.6 tons ha−1. Approximately 40.3% of the total growing stock (across all
land types) in terms of AGB is located in the natural regenerated forests, followed by
the Other land – Cultivated (28.5%). Growing stock is high in cultivated lands due to the
fact that a considerable number of TOF are an important part of the landscape in this
land category.
The mean figures (per hectare) show the highest stocking in natural regenerated forest
land. The built-up land type contains a considerable number of trees per hectare when
compared to the other land classes. The trees assigned to inland water are the ones
collected in non-perennial rivers or riverine vegetation sites. These sampling plots
contained also a remarkable number of trees in the data.
