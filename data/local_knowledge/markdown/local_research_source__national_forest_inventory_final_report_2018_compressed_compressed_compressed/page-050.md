2.11           Deadwood
 For the fallen deadwood volume, De Vries44 formula given below was used. This formula
 requires the length of transect and the log diameter at the point of intersection, as follows:




 V = volume per hectare of deadwood;
 d = log diameter at the point of intersection of the transect perpendicular to the axis of the
 log;
 L = length of transect.

Two decomposition classes are recorded for deadwood particles: sound and rotten. Be- cause
a rotten wood is lighter than a sound wood, the dry wood density of deadwood is scaled down
using lower wood densities than for live trees, as follows:

      Sound deadwood WD: 90% * Default WD;
      Rotten deadwood WD: 50% * Default WD.




 44     De Vries, 1986.
