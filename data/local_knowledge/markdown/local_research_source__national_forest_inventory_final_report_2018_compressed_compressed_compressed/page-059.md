3.2             Tree density
This section includes the results for the number of trees per hectare in the FRA classes,
LUCCs and biomes. The mean numbers of trees per ha in forests and outside of forest is
124.7 and 21.1 respectivey.

3.2.1           Tree density by forest and non-forest

On average, forests have a tree density of 124.7 trees ha−1, while non-forests have a
tree density of 21.1 trees ha−1. When tree densities are examined by LUCC types (Table
A3.1), the highest densities are located in semi-deciduous (147.9 trees ha−1) and
evergreen forests (124.9 trees ha−1). An ongoing inventory in planted forests will
provide more reliable data on tree densities in planted forests LUCC.

Table 3.3       Number of trees per hectare in Forest and Non-forest lands
            FRA class                 N of trees (N ha−1 )
              Forest                         124.7
            Non-forest                        21.1


3.2.2           Tree density by biomes

The highest mean density by biomes (containing all land types) is in Moist
Afromontane biome (76.4 trees ha−1), followed by Combretum-Terminalia (55.9) and
Acacia- Commiphora (46.0).

3.2.3           Tree density by diameter classes

Tree densities by DBH classes typically follow a reversed J-shape curve in natural forests46.
Therefore the highest number of trees is found in the smaller DBH classes. Big trees (DBH
≥ 40cm) constitute 16% of all trees in the forest land.
