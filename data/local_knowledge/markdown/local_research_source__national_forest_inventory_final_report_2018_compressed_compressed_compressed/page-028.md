Ethiopia started the second phase of REDD+ readiness in January 2013, which is focused
on implementation. The Governments of Norway and the United Kingdom provided
additional funding of USD 10 million for the implementation of the R-PP. In 2013,

Ethiopia submitted its first FREL in 2016 on a voluntary basis as for the Decision 12/
CP.17, paragraph 1323. It was the first FREL submission from the Africa region. The
FREL submission is in the context of results-based payments for the implementation of
reducing emissions from deforestation and forest degradation, sustainable
management of forests and enhancement of forest carbon stocks under UNFCCC.
The Emission Factors for this first version were calculated using first phase NFI data

country. An adjusted revision calculated using NFI data from the entire country was

1.7           Considerations on the NFI results
The data collected during the NFI were processed to produce results that describe the
state of forests and trees outside forests at the country level, and by biome. Because the
NFI was initially designed to produce results primarily at the country level (and
particularly per NFI strata), all the attempts to downscale the results can reduce the
accuracy, as for the case of the results presented per region.
The area estimates presented in this report are based on the analysis of the area
proportion within the field samples and therefore cannot substitute the results
obtained by using wall-to- wall or extensive remote sensing techniques.
The area estimates provide an overview and the optimal way to use them is to present
them per hectare so as to obtain proportions between the land use/ land cover
categories and FRA classes.
The results presented in this report are consistent with the Forest Reference Level
document submitted to UNFCCC in January 2016 as the emission factor calculation
followed the same procedure. The difference is that in this analysis, all SUs have been
computed and not just those in forestlands.
