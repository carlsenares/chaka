 2.2               Data collection
Data collection was organized in the field at different levels and includes data from
observations, measurements and interviews. The variables collected through interviews with
the local key informants are considered as qualitative data and mainly include general
descriptions and general socio-economic data. The variables collected through field
observations include LULC description, environmental problems, forest management etc.
Measured variables are the ones recorded with the support of specific tools and comprise
linear distances and orientation and classic dendrometric data including diameter at the
breast height (DBH), tree height, slope, etc. The first section of the field forms is dedicated to
the general description of the plot, location, access path and basic socioeconomic description
of the nearest village/town. The second section is specific for each plot, and it includes data
about locations, photo orientation and measurements. For all the trees and stumps measured,
the following variables are collected:
 • Position in the plot;
 • Tree/stump;
 • Species name (scientific names and vernacular names);
 • Diameter at 0.3 m level;
 • DBH and top height (for trees and stumps greater or equal DBH 10 cm in outside forest
   and greater or equal to DBH 20 cm in forest) ;
 • Bole height;
 • Stem quality;
 • Tree Health;
 • Causative agents;
 • Decomposition status.
