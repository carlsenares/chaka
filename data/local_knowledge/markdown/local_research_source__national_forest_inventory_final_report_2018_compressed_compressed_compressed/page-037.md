At every Measurement Point (in the center of each Rectangular Sub Plot) the
     following variables are recorded:
     •   Land Use/Cover Section (LUCS);
     •   Slope/orientation;
     •   Organic layer thickness;
     •   Soil drainage.
     For the small trees (DBH<10cm) in the circular subplot, species name are
     collected and the trees counted. Data on shrubs and bushes is collected in the
     RSP, including the following variables:
     •   Species;
     •   Number of stems;
     •   Average stem diameter;
     •   Average height.
     There are three transects of 10 meters long in each plot (i.e., 12 transects per
     SU) for the collection of the Deadwood including quality and decomposition
     status. Characteristic of each LUCS is observed and described in a specific
     section including:
     •   Land Use/Land Cover (LULC) type;
     •   Soil erosion;
     •   Length of the section (inside the plot);
     •   Vegetation cover;
     •   Environmental problems (including disturbances);
     •   Management status.

     Extensive training events were organized in order to secure that the field
     crews correctly collect the field data. During the first phase of the field
     campaign from March to September 2014, four teams were trained and
     engaged. Two additional field teams were added in September 2014. From
     February until August 2016, two more teams were trained and added. At the
     end of the NFI field campaign, total of eight teams were operational in the field.
     Each team was equipped with the following tools to correctly record the data:
     GPS receiver, compass, camera, caliper, meter tapes, laser range finder, and
     clinometer.
