2               Methodology
2.1             Sampling design
The NFI sampling design was based on a) available map layers which were then used
for pre-stratification, b) the available budget and on c) human resources available for
the assessment.
The sampling design was constructed to enable periodic monitoring, ensuring
statistical reliability of the collected data in the future. Using available geospatial layers
of Ethiopia (including Agro-Ecological Zones (AEZ), Potential Vegetation Map (PVM),
and Digital Elevation Model (DEM)), the whole country was first classified into five
strata. In order to finalize the strata definitions, the forest inventory team conducted a
series of consultative meetings with forestry experts, plant ecologists, statisticians and
the National MRV Task Force where past experiences on forest inventories was
reviewed and discussed. The implementation of the stratification for the systematic
sampling design started by overlapping the layer of the AEZ of Ethiopia with the data
produced from the WBISPP.
In this first stage, four strata were obtained. A supplementary stratum was included in
order to identify the differences between the woodland ecology that encompasses the
widest part of North and South western and eastern parts of the country. The
woodland stratum was therefore further split into two in order to obtain comparable
sampling intensities. The final stratification is therefore composed of five strata that
give a macro- ecological characterization of the vegetation of Ethiopia (Table 2.1).

Table 2.1       Strata used classes used for the NFI sampling
              Strata                                            Description
            Stratum I                           Natural forest and plantation and Bamboo
                                       North and South Eastern part of the woodland mainly Acacia-
            Stratum II
                                            Commiphora woodland of Somali and Afar region.
            Stratum III                  North and South Western woodland part of Terminalia-

            Stratum IV                                       Other land
            Stratum V                                         Desert (Beriha)

The sampling design chosen is stratified systematic cluster24 sampling with different
sampling intensities by strata: stratum I has a grid of 8 minutes in latitude and 8
minutes in longitude, stratum II has sampling units every 30 minutes in latitude and 30
minutes in longitude and strata III and IV have sampling units every 25 minutes in
latitude and 25 minutes in longitude due to their seamless nature in terms of landscape
mosaic. Stratum V includes sampling units every 60 minutes in latitude and 25 minutes
in longitude. A final number of 631 sampling units were identified. All the SUs falling in

24     Clusters are called as Sampling Unit (SU) in the Ethiopian NFI.
