Specific objectives of the Ethiopian NFI were:
     •   The identification of a methodological approach to support the Measuring,
         Reporting and Verification (MRV) implementation by taking into account the
         national context through a participatory process;
     • The design and set up of for a long-term monitoring system of the forests and trees
       outside of forest based on a network of permanent plots located across the country;
     • The collection of variables that respond to the national data needs;
     • The establishment of a database to store and manage the field information for
       analysis and dissemination.
     The forest definition adopted for NFI is: Forest as a land with of 10% tree canopy cover, a
     0.5 ha area and a 5 m tree height in average.


                      Methodology
     The sampling design chosen for NFI is stratified systematic cluster sampling. Using
     available geospatial layers of Ethiopia and large scale ecological studies the whole
     country was classified into five strata that give a macro ecological characterization of
     five macro areas. The number of the sampling unitsvarieswithin the strata depending on
     the estimated probability to find trees and forests. A total of 627 sampling units were
     established across the five strata. Every sampling unit has an area of 1 km2 and is
     composed of 4 plots (with cumulative plot area of 2 ha).
     A series of variables were chosen to give a complete characterization of Ethiopian
     forests, including biomass, carbon, biodiversity, health status and disturbances. The
     quality of the final results is ensured by rigorous Quality Assessment/Quality Control
     (QA/QC) procedures.
     The methodology adopted can be replicated as all the sampling units are permanent
     and the data collection can be improved according to the findings on this first field
     campaign. All the collected NFI data has been stored in a database at the MRV
     laboratory of the MEFCC.
     In 2015 the stratification scheme was changed because Ethiopia decided to adopt a
     classification that better describes the vegetation characteristics of the country. The
     following biomes were adopted:

               Acacia-Commiphora
               Combretum-Terminalia
               Dry Afromontane
               Moist Afromontane
     This change resulted in the adoption of more specific analysis methods. All the results
     are thus presented by biome, and not by original NFI strata. All results have been
     computed using general models, formulas and conversion factors described in this
     report.
