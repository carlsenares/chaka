Three sets of subplots are delimited within each plot corresponding to different data
collection levels:
   • 3 Rectangular Sub Plot (RSP) 10 m x 20 m (200 m2), corresponding to level 1;
   • 3 Circular Sub Plot (CSP), with a radius of 3.99 m (50 m2), corresponding to level 2,
      located in the left-hand half of the rectangular subplots;
   • 3 Litter Sub Plot (LSP), also circular but smaller with a radius of 18 cm (about 0.1 m2),
      corresponding to level 3, located in the centre of the CSPs.

All these subplot categories are numbered from 1 to 3, from the starting point of the plot to
the end of the plot. An edaphic (soil) and topographic Measurement point (MP) is established
at the centre of each rectangular subplot. A Fallen Deadwood Transect (FDT) is located at the
end of each rectangular subplot. Each plot is divided into LUCS representing homogeneous
land use / vegetation cover portions of land (e.g. forest, crops, grassland), with variable section
size and shape identified in the field. The classification system adopted to identify the
different land use/cover classes is described in appendix (A1.1). Relevant data related to
grazing, cropping and forest characteristics, management and resources use and users are
collected within the LUCS. Figure 2.1 shows the distribution of SUs in the field and Figure 2.2
shows the structure of the SU, plots and sub plots.

The land use/cover classes (LUCC) classification includes different levels as follows:

   1. The first level is based on the classification system developed by the FRA Programme
      of FAO to ensure harmonization between countries for regional or global assessments.
      These global FRA classes consist of Forests, Other Wooded Land, Other Land and
      Inland Water.
   2. The second, third and fourth levels are country specific, and include additional classes
      that meet specific federal and regional information needs.
   3. The second level applies to all classes and differentiates between land use/land cover.
   4. The third level refers to evergreen, semi deciduous and deciduous forests and describes
      its naturalness.
   5. The fourth level mainly applies to vegetation canopy cover and is applicable only to
      evergreen forest, shrubs and natural grassland types.

   A full description of the LUCCs is presented in Appendix A1.
