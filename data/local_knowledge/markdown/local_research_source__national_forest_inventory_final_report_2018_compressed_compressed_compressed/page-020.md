1 Introduction
Ethiopia’s vision and strategy for CRGE2 is articulated in its vision document endorsed
by the parliament at the end of 2011. This initiative, comprising a strategy for climate
resilient development and another for a green economy, seeks to improve resilience to
climate change, ensure abatement, enhance avoidance of future emissions, as well as
foster both economic development and less carbon dependent growth. The strategy
focuses on adapting to climate change to minimize the potential risks and to maximize
the potential benefits.
The country’s commitments on developing its green economy include the protection and
re-establishment of forests and their economic and ecosystem services, including the
reduction of emissions from deforestation and forest degradation. In this context,
Ethiopia has chosen the REDD+ initiative as a way to mitigate climate change through the
forestry sector by implementing activities that should be measured, reported and verified
through an appropriate national forest monitoring system.
The Government of Ethiopia, in partnership with the World Bank’s FCPF and the
BioCarbon Fund, identified the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) of the United
Nations as a qualified partner to provide the necessary Technical Assistance (TA) to
address the lack of monitoring capacities and experiences in applying international
standards through capacity transfer, capacity building and awareness on the various
components of its national forest MRV system.
Ethiopia decided in this context to implement its first NFI with the aim of constructing
a countrywide scenario of the forest, for the generation of reliable information on
forest resources to be used as a baseline for policy formulation, institutional capacity
building, planning, conservation and utilization of natural resources on a sustainable
basis.
The implementation of the NFI started in 2013 as a FAO-TCP project to pilot the
National Forest Inventory and was then scaled up with the aid of FCPF and BioCarbon
Fund for broader implementation of REDD+ Readiness at MEFCC through a Technical
Agreement. FAO was tasked with supporting the implementation of a national forest
monitoring and MRV system for REDD+ readiness in Ethiopia. The government was
supported by FAO in addressing the lack of monitoring capacities and experience in
applying international standards in the framework of REDD+ through capacity building
and awareness raising on reference/baseline scenario construction and MRV systems as
well as the establishment of a system for monitoring forest area change.
The field campaign and the results of the NFI are described in this report. This was the
first NFI campaign in Ethiopia and it was carried out during the period from March
2013 to November 2016.
