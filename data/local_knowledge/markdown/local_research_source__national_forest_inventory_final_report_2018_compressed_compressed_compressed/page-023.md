     In the Horn of Africa, Ethiopia is recognized as the center of diversity and
     endemism for several plant species (6,000 species of higher plants of which 10%
     are endemic9). Woody plant species constitute about 1,000 species in high natural
     forests, woodlands, plantation forests, and bamboo forests.
     According to the Vegetation Map of Africa10 Ethiopia is located in three floristic
     regions, the Afromontane Region (in the Ethiopia Highlands), the Sudanian
     Region, and the Somalia- Masai Region (southern Ethiopia excluding the
     mountains). The Afromontane Region occurring mainly in the Ethiopian
     Highlands contains undifferentiated woodland and country specific peculiarities
     like the transition from undifferentiated Ethiopian woodland to Afromontane (i.e.
     single-dominant Juniperus procera areas). These differences are important in
     order to accept the key indicator characterization assigned by the co- prominence
     presence of Podocarpus falcatus. This kind of single species prominence is also
     described in other locations as in Afromontane single-dominant Hagenia
     abyssinica forest and Afromontane moist transitional forests.
     The Sudanian Region is a natural continuity of the Afromontane Region (north-
     west), and it is located in the lowland normally below the 750 m a.s.l. It mainly
     includes areas that are called Ethiopian undifferentiated woodland. The
     dominating tree genera are Anogeissus, Balanites, Combretum, Commiphora and
     Terminalia.
     From the steppe slopes of the Afromontane region (south-east) starts the
     Somalia-Masai region. It is covered with bushes and thickets, and above them
     deciduous species which grades into a semi-evergreen and evergreen vegetation
     of the same type in the lower slopes of the mountains. This region belongs mainly
     to the Acacia-Commiphora type and it includes different vegetation types such as
     scrub forest, riparian forest, secondary grassland and wooded grassland,
     seasonally waterlogged grassland, semi-desert grassland and shrubland, and
     desert. A more precise characterization of the forest vegetation of Ethiopia is
     presented by Friis et al. (2010), where the forest types are identified by taking
     into account the bioclimatic regions, as follows:
1. Upland dry evergreen forest of Eritrea and on the eastern escarpment (including
   A. abyssinica in the south part and Barbeya oleoides and Tarchonantus
   camphoratus in the northern part);
2. Upland dry evergreen forest of the plateau (mainly Juniperus sp., Podocarpus sp.
   and Olea sp.);
3. Upland dry evergreen forest of Sudamo, Bale and Harerge (J. procera and B.
   oleoides); Upland Rain Forests;
4. Humid mixed forest around the southern part of the Rift Valley and in Harerge
   (broadleaves and evergreen);
