Executive Summary
In the first quarter of 2016, the Government of Ethiopia (GoE) announced that the
number of people affected by severe drought caused by El Niño stood at 10.2 million in
the period 2015/2016.
Ethiopia is seriously committed to addressing climate change. The Climate Resilient
Green Economy (CRGE) strategy that was officially introduced during the 17th
Conference of the Parties (COP 17) in Durban in 2011 is a clear manifestation of
commitment to pursue a carbon-neutral development regime. The CRGE strategy has
identified a number of low carbon-emitting initiatives across key economic sectors
(forestry, agriculture, energy, livestock, transport and industry) that will help Ethiopia
achieve its vision of building a climate-resilient middle-income economy by 2025.The
successful implementation of these initiatives would help Ethiopia eliminate projected
emissions of over 250 Mt CO2e a year by 2030 compared to the actual Business as
Usual (BAU) scenario. The strategy aims to maintain the annual Greenhouse Gas (GHG)
level of the country at the 2010’s level, i.e., 150 Mt CO2e.
The forests in the country are under direct and indirect threats. Direct threats include
small and large-scale agricultural land conversion, increased wood extraction for fuel
and construction, and pressure caused by increased livestock grazing. The indirect
threats comprise gaps in the application of forest policy and regulations; tenure/unclear
forest user rights; lack of private investment in forestry development; population
growth; and inadequate land use planning and participatory forest management (PFM).
As a result, the forestry sector is the second largest contributor of GHG emissions in the
country after agriculture. Under BAU, greater pressure on forests will increase the rate
of deforestation and forest degradation and prevent the country from reaping its long-
term environmental and economic benefits.
In this context the knowledge of the status of the forest and the Trees Outside of Forest
(TOF) is a country priority. The CRGE strategy includes the adoption of the REDD+
programme as operational instrument for mitigate the CO2 emissions in the forest sector.


              Objectives
Ethiopia conducted the National Forest Inventory (NFI) field campaign between 2014
and 2016. The NFI started as a FAO - Technical Co-operation Project (TCP) in 2013 with
the aim to pilot and test the National Forest Inventory methodology, and was then
scaled up with the aid of Forest Carbon Partnership Facility (FCPF) and Bio-Carbon
Fund for broader implementation of Reducing Emissions from Deforestation and
Forest Degradation (REDD+) Readiness at the Ministry of Environment, Forest and
Climate Change (MEFCC) in late 2014 and continued with the FAO technical support.
