1.5           Objectives and Scopes of the
              National Forest Inventory
The implementation of NFI started in 2013 as a FAO-TCP project to pilot the NFI and
was then scaled up with the aid of FCPF and BioCarbon Fund for broader
implementation of REDD+ Readiness at MEFCC. The support included the
establishment of a MRV unit and it focused on implementing, in particular, Component
4 of the REDD Readiness Wheel (RRW): Design a Monitoring System for Emissions and
Removals and Other Benefits and Impacts by providing the monitoring tools required
to measure the impact and revert the effects of this pressure through its main outcome
of Ethiopia’s MRV capacity to implement REDD+ strengthened. The objectives for a
complete assessment of a Monitoring System for Emissions and Removals include the
following:
1. The identification of a methodological approach developed by the FAO REDD+ team
   to support MRV implementation taking into account the national context through a
   participatory process.
2. The design and set up of a long-term monitoring system of the forests and trees
   outside of forest based on a network of permanent plots located across the country.
3. The collection of variables that respond to national data needs mainly forest and
   other land cover area, growing stock, biomass and carbon, state and quality of the
   forests and trees outside of forests, social economic information about use and
   management of forest products and services.
4. The establishment of a database to store and manage the field information for
   analysis and dissemination.
From 2013 to 2016 Ethiopia reached new steps on the REDD+ implementation and the
MRV Unit was established at MEFCC. In January 2016, MEFCC with the support of FAO
submitted the first FREL report to United Nations Framework Convention on Climate
Change (UNFCCC). This document helps the country to respect commitments on the
threshold in CO2 emissions in order to access the result based payments taking into
account:
• The reducing of the emissions from deforestation;
• The conservation of forest carbon stocks; and
• The enhancement of forest carbon stocks.
The Emission Factors for the FREL reporting have been calculated using solely the
current NFI data.
