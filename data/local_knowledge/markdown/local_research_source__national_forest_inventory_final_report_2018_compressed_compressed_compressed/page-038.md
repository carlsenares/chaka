Procedures to improve precision in the measurements
On the basis of the partial data set collected, a possible cause of uncertainty was
identified in the measurement procedures. A series of best practices on the
importance of data collection (including double measurement) were compiled and
explained to the experts in the field in order to increase the accuracy of the
measurements. Three critical tree attributes subject to errors were identified: DBH,
height and scientific names. In the dendrometric measurements, height is the
variable subject to most common errors. Using the clinometer as a height
measurement tool, tree height is measured using the distance between the observer
and the tree bole and the angle obtained between the ground slope, the observer
eyes, and the top of the tree. The height has a considerable influence on the final
Above Ground Biomass estimates25.
