3.9                Tree species and biodiversity
A total of 360 tree species has been identified during the data collection and the quality
control operations. The tree species have been then ranked following two different
approaches: species names are sorted in descending order of total frequency and by
total stem volume. The first criterion requires counting the total number of stems by
species in the category of interest, and the second one is calculation of the total tree
volumes accordingly. Besides the ranking, the Shannon index estimates are computed.
The unknown species have not been included in the analysis. However, unknown
species represent approximately 20% of the total number of trees observed in the NFI.
Figures 3.13 and 3.14 show the 30 most abundant tree species and the 30tree species
with highest volume stock in Ethiopia, respectively.
