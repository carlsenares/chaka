4              Conclusions and way
               forward
The Ethiopian NFI was designed by following the FAO-NFMA methodology with
technical support provided by FAO. The objective of the NFI is to obtain countrywide
baseline information for the forest sector. The NFI was further enriched when the
country decided to participate in the REDD+ mechanism. NFI is an essential part of the
national forest monitoring and MRV systems for REDD+. Conducting the NFI helped
Ethiopia to increase its capacity and gain valuable experiences in implementing MRV of
forest resources through extensive capacity building and awareness raising of topics
such as reference/baseline scenario construction, NFI design and implementation,
monitoring of forest area change, statistics, data processing and reporting. More than
150 Ethiopian technicians have received training in data collection, analysis and
reporting methods at the federal and regional levels during the project.
The NFI and MRV systems will allow the Government to assess its own progress in
reducing emissions from deforestation and degradation and in increasing forest carbon
stocks. In addition, a strengthened forest information database and improved capacity
will place Ethiopia in a better position for formulation of policy for sustainable forest
management, implementing best practices in forest management and establishing a
complete national forest assessment monitoring system. Thus, the NFI provides
baseline information that can support Ethiopia to improve management of its lands
and particularly forest lands, to improve the strategic planning of the fuel wood
production and to further improve the national and subnational forest information
systems. Since the NFI collected data in all land types, including also areas outside of
forests, the NFI results will give a preliminary but exhaustive overview on the status of
trees outside of forests at the national and subnational levels too.
The NFI was designed to provide information for REDD+, and therefore new methods,
tools and collected data stemming from this initiative have improved the knowledge on
current forest carbon stock, emission factor(s) and other relevant forest information on
forest use and forest cover. The information derived from the NFI was used to calculate
and report the emission factors for the first Ethiopian forest reference level submission in
2016.
In 27 months of field data collection, a total of 49,400 trees were recorded in 1,029 plots in
539 sampling units in all the regions of the country. The results show that the population
pressure is high, because 65.8% of forest lands are somehow disturbed and 58.8% of
OWLs are disturbed by human activity. In forest land the main cause of forest
disturbance is encroachment by agriculture. Results on the basal area in forest lands also
indicate that forests appear to be degraded, with BA in forests13.3 m2ha-1 and in High
woodland only 3.8 m2ha-1. The basal area results by biome (forest only plots), in Acacia-
Commiphora, Combretum-Terminalia, Dry Afromontane and Moist Afromontane
estimates are 6.5, 8.4, 12.4 and 20.7 m2ha-1, respectively. Only Moist Afromontane
forest has a basal area greater than 20 m2ha-1.
