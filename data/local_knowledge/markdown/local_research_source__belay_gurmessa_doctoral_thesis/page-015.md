ABSTRACT

Small-scale farmers in poor countries, particularly in Africa, that depend on rain-fed crops for a
living have been impacted by climate change. Climate change adaptation and mitigation are
critical in this environment. This study was to assess the impact of climate change and
adaptation strategies among smallholder farmers in Chiro Woreda. The selection of the kebeles
were done purposefully based on the agroecology. For the study, one group in each kebeles was
formed composed of male and female to collect primary data. The main methods used to gather
the data were interviews with kebeles administrators’, woreda managers’, and development team
leaders’ peoples and local elders; questionnaires; focused group discussions (FGDs) with
selected farmers; and reviewing secondary data from different unpublished sources. Descriptive
ways of analysis methods was applied. Farmers had adopted various climate change
adaptations; the respondents had income source diversification; early and late planting, shifting
from cattle to small ruminants, practicing soil and water conservation, having planted drought-
resistant crops, practicing decreased livestock, practicing forestation and reforestation,
practicing mixed farming, practicing irrigation, and practicing daily labor. The study was
focused on major problems of crop production. Farmers also sorted out their major challenges
for their failures to adapt, which include lack of irrigation, inadequate of money to finance their
adaptation strategies, inadequate of knowledge concerning appropriate adaptation strategies,
unavailable of information about long-term climate change, lack of extension services,
insufficient infrastructure, shortage of land, insecure property rights and land tenure issues, and
lack of credit service.

Key words:, climate change, adaptation strategy, adaptation, smallholder farmers.
