The primary climate-related risks to smallholder farmers were high temperatures, droughts, brief
downpours, livestock deaths, crop diseases, human illnesses, and unpredictable rainfall. When
asked to rank the primary climate hazards that smallholder farmers face, 121 respondents, or
90.9%, indicated that high temperatures were the top hazard. Other top-ranked hazards included
drought (86.5%), very short rain (82.7%), livestock death (68.4%), human diseases (60.15%),
crop diseases (52.6%), erratic rain fall (45.8%), heavy rainfall (15%), land degradation (11.27%),
and heavy hailstorm (8.27%).
