5.3 Recommendation
The study findings indicate that certain household and farm-level factors significantly increase
farmers' adoption of climate change adaptation measures, including: more labor capacity, higher
frequency of extension service visits, greater farming experience, and more resources and assets.
Based on these insights, the passage suggests that policies aimed at improving these factors
could help bolster on-farm climate change adaptation among smallholder farmers.

In contrast, the findings show that lack of mass media and credit access discourage climate
change adaptation. Therefore, may need to re-examine and reform policies in these areas to make
them more effective tools for supporting private climate adaptation.
