not being flooded at high tides.

The natural vegetation of the plain is hard to be reconstructed, as with its
development also human settlement began. On the slightly sandy ridges
Acacia-Savannah is assumed to be the natural vegetation, dominated by Acacia
abyssinica which today can only be found scarcely and in small populations.
This potential forest-site now is populated by Eucalypts in large areas as they
ensure the survival of human culture after the clearance of acacia forests.
