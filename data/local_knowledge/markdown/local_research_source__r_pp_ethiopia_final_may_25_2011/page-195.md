                     Recommendations for REDD strategy development
                     The recommendations in order of popularity focussed on:
                         Ensuring that forest affected community members have a say in how
                            the REDD strategy is developed.
                         Strengthening/developing an institution responsible for forestry
                            which is adapted to be able to manage REDD implementation.
                         Developing regulations and supporting the implementation of existing
                            forest policies.
                         Revising the existing policies that impact on forestry to strengthen
                            local user rights and focus on enhancing benefits from the forest to
                            local people.
                         Rather than start new projects, build REDD support onto existing
                            successful strategies such as Participatory Forest Management.

                     Risks to REDD implementation.
                          Insufficient benefits going to forest communities, imbalance between
                             burdens and benefits.
                          Population growth, food insecurity, etc. creating pressures on forest
                             that REDD incentives can not halt.
                          Raising too high expectations that are not met will lead to
                             disillusionment among affected stakeholders.
Stakeholder          In this preliminary stakeholder analysis 16 different categories of
identification and   stakeholders were identified. Stakeholders were defined as groups/types of
analysis             people either affected by or influential on forestry decisions. The
                     stakeholder groups were mapped out in terms of their current
                     affectedness/accountability and power/influence over forestry decisions and
                     shifts were identified that were required to make forestry decision making
                     more democratic during the REDD strategy development phase. Full results
                     are contained in the Annex of this component.

                           The most notable recommended power shifts during the REDD
                            strategy development phase included providing more access to
                            decision making for forest communities and forest fringe
                            communities and also private sector forest industries/enterprises
                            (large and small, formal and informal).
                           The Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development(MoARD),
                            Environmental Protection Authority(EPA) and the government bodies
                            responsible for culture/tourism and wildlife were pointed out as
                            organisations that could enhance participation in decision making
                            during the REDD strategy development phase and thus become more
                            accountable.

                     The suggested mechanisms to enhance participation during the REDD
                     strategy development phase included the following:

                           Raise awareness on the rationale, principles and mechanisms for
                            participation – both at high and low levels.
